# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Mixed audience: both technical (junior developers learning LLM internals) and non-technical readers (sales, marketing, ops staff) being onboarded to LLM literacy. The progressive complexity across 6 sessions accommodates both tracks without alienating either.

## Product Purpose

A single-page educational site that demystifies large language models through 6 interactive, scroll-driven sessions. Each session covers one LLM concept (tokenization, attention, context, RAG, prompting, MCP) with analogies, interactive demos, and visual explanations. Success is a reader finishing all 6 sessions with genuine understanding they couldn't get from a blog post alone.

## Brand Personality

Playful, vivid, bold. The site should feel alive and engaging, not sterile or textbook-like. Each session gets its own accent color, reflecting energy and visual variety. The dark canvas with saturated colors creates contrast and excitement. Confident without being arrogant; fun without being childish.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages with white cards, blue hero gradients, and stock-photo vibes
- Overly playful or kitschy designs that feel childish rather than engaging
- Dense academic layouts with walls of text and no visual breaks
- The AI-slop template: identical card grids, gradient heroes, glassmorphism everywhere

## Design Principles

- **Progressive revelation**: each session builds on the last; the UI supports this narrative arc with visual variety per session
- **Show, don't tell**: interactive demos and visual analogies over paragraphs of explanation
- **One concept at a time**: each session owns one idea; never overload a section
- **Vivid but not chaotic**: bold accent colors per session within a consistent dark framework
- **Respect the reader**: assume intelligence, explain complexity, never condescend

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Standard accessibility is sufficient: sufficient contrast ratios, keyboard navigability, meaningful alt text on visuals, and respect for reduced-motion preferences. No specific user accommodations beyond the baseline.
